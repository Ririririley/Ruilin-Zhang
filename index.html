Rules of the game:

Suppose there is a company that manufactures smartphones and the initial corporate score is 60.
Players participating in the game are required to draw cards of characters representing different stakeholders and are required to answer 8 questions (4 of which are specific and 4 of which are common).
Depending on the choices made, this will result in an increase or decrease in the Enterprise Score.
At the end of the game,
if the business score is less than 60, the business is bankrupt;
if the business score is greater than 60 and less than 80, the business is mediocre;
and if the business score is greater than 80, the business is way ahead.

The aim of the game is to prompt players to think about how to implement responsible innovation across different stakeholders.

Here's a sample video：

CEO:

Question 1:In the face of global competition, how do you decide on the company's expansion strategy?

Question 2:When one of the company's products has a severe safety issue that has already harmed consumers, how would you handle it?

Question 3:When faced with a decision that requires balancing company profits with social responsibility, what is your choice?

Question 4:A serious ethical violation occurs within the company, and you learn that a senior manager might be involved. How will you handle it?

Consumers:

Question 1:You purchased a new smartphone but found that its battery life is far less than described in the advertisements. What would you do?

Question 2:You notice that a brand's product packaging uses a significant amount of non-recyclable materials. What's your choice?

Question 3:While using a product, you feel that some of its features could be further optimized to meet consumer needs. What would you do?

Question 4:You read online reports about a brand's unethical business practices, which raises concerns about their products. What's your decision?

Employees:

Question 1:You discover that a managerial decision in the company might result in unfair treatment to other colleagues. What would you do?

Question 2:You notice that some of the company's workflows are inefficient, potentially affecting the overall productivity. However, pointing out these observations means the team might need to undergo significant changes. What would you do?

Question 3:The company plans to enter a new market that you believe has potential risks. You think this decision could affect the company's stability and profitability. What's your decision?

Question 4:You discover that the company's product might be controversial or misunderstood in a certain cultural context, potentially affecting the company's reputation and sales in that market. However, bringing up these observations might cause project delays. What would you do?
